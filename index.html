About

I am an Assistant Professor of Data Science and Computer Science at Saint Mary’s College of California. My research focuses on responsible artificial intelligence, human–AI interaction, Bayesian modeling, cognitive modeling, and the role of AI in education and decision-making.

My current work investigates how users interpret AI-generated outputs, how bias in large language models can be measured, and how transparency tools can support more informed human–AI interaction.

Research

Responsible AI and LLM Bias

We study how bias appears in large language model outputs and how users respond when bias information is presented at the point of use. This work combines behavioral experiments, statistical modeling, and responsible AI design.

Bayesian Bias Aggregation

We develop Bayesian methods for combining bias ratings from multiple large language models and human evaluators. The goal is to produce bias scores with uncertainty estimates rather than relying on a single deterministic label.

AI in Data Science Education

We explore how AI tools can support student learning in statistics, data analysis, and programming, while maintaining active learning, critical thinking, and academic integrity.

Cognitive and Agent-Based Modeling

This research area focuses on modeling human behavior, personality, trust, learning, and interaction using computational and statistical models.
